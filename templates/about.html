Health Parameters Analyzed

Glucose Level

Blood glucose levels are the primary indicator of diabetes. Elevated fasting glucose levels (≥126 mg/dL) suggest impaired glucose metabolism and are a key diagnostic criterion for diabetes.

BMI (Body Mass Index)

BMI is a key indicator of obesity, which is one of the strongest risk factors for type 2 diabetes. Higher BMI values (≥25) significantly increase diabetes risk.

Age

Age is a significant risk factor as the prevalence of diabetes increases with age, particularly after 45 years. The risk continues to rise with advancing age.

Blood Pressure

Hypertension is commonly associated with diabetes and metabolic syndrome. High blood pressure (≥130/80) can indicate insulin resistance and increased diabetes risk.

Technical Details

Our simplified prediction model is built using state-of-the-art machine learning techniques:

  • XGBoost Algorithm: An advanced gradient boosting framework optimized for medical prediction tasks
  • Feature Selection: Uses only the 4 most clinically relevant parameters for simplicity and accuracy
  • Data Preprocessing: StandardScaler for feature normalization to ensure consistent predictions
  • Class Balancing: SMOTETomek technique to handle imbalanced dataset and improve prediction accuracy
